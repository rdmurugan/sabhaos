# Customer interview guide — template

The highest-leverage 30 minutes you can spend on CX work. Run 5-10 of these quarterly. Replace any survey with a conversation if you can.

---

## Pre-interview prep

```
□ Who are you talking to? (Name, role, company, tenure as customer)
□ Why this person? (Specific segment, recent behavior, key account)
□ What do you want to learn? (Specific question or hypothesis)
□ Calendar 30 min (some go to 45 if they're engaged)
□ Recording? (Get consent; offer to skip recording if they prefer)
```

**Pick interviewees deliberately.** Not just available people; representative of the segment you're trying to understand.

---

## The opening (3 min)

```
"Thanks for taking the time. We're trying to understand how [our product] 
fits into your workflow / business / day, and what could be better. This 
isn't a sales call — I'm genuinely trying to learn from you. Is it okay 
if I record this just for my notes? [If yes, start recording.]

A few ground rules:
- There are no wrong answers; we want your honest perspective.
- Feel free to be critical; constructive criticism is the most valuable thing.
- I'll listen more than I talk; I want your story, not my pitch.
- We'll wrap in about 30 minutes."
```

---

## Core questions (20 min)

Pick 5-7 of these depending on your goals:

### To understand value / fit

```
□ Walk me through how you discovered [our product]. What was happening?
□ What problem were you trying to solve?
□ What did you do before [our product] existed for you?
□ What was your first impression? What surprised you (good or bad)?
□ How do you describe what we do to a peer who hasn't heard of us?
```

### To understand activation / onboarding

```
□ Walk me through your first week using us. What worked? What didn't?
□ When did you know this was worth the time / money?
□ What's something you wish you'd known when you started?
□ Did anything almost make you give up?
```

### To understand retention / habit

```
□ What's the moment in your week / day when you reach for [our product]?
□ How has your usage changed over time?
□ Are there features you use heavily? Features you've never used?
□ Is there a competitor or alternative tool that comes up in your team?
```

### To understand churn risk

```
□ Have you ever considered stopping using us? What was happening then?
□ What would have to be true for you to switch to a competitor?
□ If we disappeared tomorrow, what would you do?
□ Is there anyone on your team who's NOT using us / wishes you weren't?
```

### To understand value perception

```
□ How do you justify the cost of [our product] to yourself or your team?
□ If you had to pay double, would you? Why or why not?
□ What's the most underrated thing about us? The most overrated?
□ What would make this 2x more valuable to you?
```

### To find unmet needs / wedge expansion

```
□ What's the most painful part of your workflow that we're NOT helping with?
□ Are there tools you use alongside us? How do they fit together?
□ Have you ever wished we did something that we don't?
□ What does "great" look like for [their job-to-be-done]?
```

---

## Close (5 min)

```
□ "Is there anything I didn't ask that you wish I had?"
□ "Anything you want to share that we didn't get to?"
□ "Can I follow up if I have a quick question?"
□ "Would you be open to a 15-minute follow-up in 3 months?"
□ "Thanks again. This is incredibly valuable for us. Anything we can do for 
   you in return?" (Genuine offer — referrals, intros, etc.)
```

**Always offer something in return.** Even just "I'll send you our roadmap thinking for the next quarter" creates reciprocity.

---

## Anti-patterns

| Pattern | Why bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Pitching mid-interview | Loses trust; they shut down | Listen mode; pitch never |
| Leading questions ("Don't you think X is great?") | They tell you what you want to hear | Open questions; let them lead |
| Multi-part questions | They answer one part, miss others | One question at a time |
| Filling silence | They might have been about to say something important | Let silence stretch 5-10 seconds |
| Defending against criticism | Loses the data | "Tell me more about that" — never defend |
| Interview by committee (3 of you + 1 of them) | Intimidating | One-on-one ideally; two max from your side |
| Forgetting to record / take notes | You'll forget the gold | Record (with consent) or have dedicated note-taker |

## Post-interview discipline

Within 24 hours of each interview:

```
□ Write a 1-page summary:
   - Who, when, context
   - 3-5 key insights
   - Direct quotes worth preserving
   - Action items (if any) for our team
   - Follow-up scheduled (if appropriate)
□ Tag insights with categories (activation, retention, feature requests, pain points)
□ Add to a central repository searchable by future product/CX team members
```

**The CX repository.** Over time, you have 50, 100, 500 conversations worth of insights. Search by category to find patterns. This is the institutional memory of your customer base.

---

## Patterns over time

After 10+ interviews, look for patterns:

```
□ Are the same 3 things coming up as pain points? → product roadmap input
□ Are the same 3 things coming up as "what they love"? → marketing material
□ Are 3+ customers asking for the same feature? → real signal to prioritize
□ Are 3+ customers describing the same workflow we don't support? → expansion opportunity
□ Are 3+ customers about to churn for the same reason? → retention initiative
```

3+ on the same theme = signal. <3 is noise.

---

## When to do this

```
□ Pre-launch: 10-20 prospect interviews to validate the wedge
□ Post-launch (first 6 months): every other week, talk to 1 new customer
□ Steady state: 5-10 customers per quarter
□ Before major decisions (roadmap, pricing, positioning): 10+ interviews specifically targeted
□ After significant churn / unexpected feedback: 5+ interviews with affected segment
```

If you haven't talked to a customer in the last 30 days, you're guessing at strategy.

---

## When customer interviews aren't enough

| Situation | What to add |
|---|---|
| Need quantitative signal | Survey of 100+ users (NPS or specific question) |
| Pre-PMF, no customers | Talk to prospects in the target segment; same questions |
| Multi-language customer base | Hire interviewers who speak the language; cultural context matters |
| Regulated industry where customers can't share | Use win-loss interviews with prospects (different rules) |
| B2B with technical buyers | Combine interviews with hands-on product walkthroughs |

---

## Operator anti-pattern: outsourcing the interviews

**Don't.** Customer interviews are too valuable to outsource:

- The questions you ask reveal your priorities (delegated, this signal is lost)
- The follow-ups reveal nuance (interviewers without product context miss this)
- The relationships matter (your customers should feel heard by you, not by a researcher)
- The strategic insights are personal (your gut + their answers reveals patterns)

Founder / CXO / product lead does customer interviews. Always.
