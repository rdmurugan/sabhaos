# Privacy policy skeleton — template

A fillable scaffold for a B2B SaaS privacy policy. Cover the standard sections. Once drafted, have a privacy attorney review before publishing — especially if EU users are in scope.

**Risk tier:** YELLOW (drafting); RED if you have an active regulatory inquiry.

**Jurisdictional scope:** this skeleton covers GDPR and CCPA/CPRA at a baseline. Other jurisdictions (UK GDPR, Brazil's LGPD, India's DPDP, Canada's PIPEDA, etc.) have additional or different requirements. Verify your full coverage with counsel.

---

## Section 1 — Identity and contact

```
PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: [YYYY-MM-DD]

This Privacy Policy explains how [COMPANY LEGAL NAME] ("[Company]," "we," "us,"
or "our") collects, uses, and shares information about you when you use our
website at [URL], our services, or otherwise interact with us.

For privacy-related inquiries, contact us at: [privacy@example.com] or 
[mailing address].

EU representatives (if you're not established in the EU but have EU users 
under GDPR Article 27): [EU rep name and contact].

UK representative (if applicable): [UK rep name and contact].
```

## Section 2 — Information we collect

Standard categories:

```
INFORMATION YOU PROVIDE

- Account information: name, email, password, company.
- Profile information: title, photo, preferences.
- Communications: support tickets, feedback, survey responses.
- Payment information: handled by [payment processor — typically Stripe]; 
  we receive limited information (last 4 digits, billing zip, etc.).

INFORMATION COLLECTED AUTOMATICALLY

- Usage data: pages visited, features used, time-on-task, click events.
- Device information: browser, OS, IP address, device type.
- Cookies and similar technologies: [link to cookie notice].

INFORMATION FROM THIRD PARTIES

- Authentication providers (Google, Microsoft, etc.) when you sign in via SSO.
- Integrations you authorize (e.g., your CRM, calendar, communication tools).
- Public sources (e.g., LinkedIn for lead enrichment, if applicable).
```

---

## Section 3 — How we use information

Tie each use to a category. Required for GDPR (Article 13 transparency).

```
We use the information we collect to:

- Provide and improve our services
- Authenticate your account
- Process payments and billing
- Communicate with you (transactional, occasional product updates)
- Send marketing communications (with your consent where required by law)
- Detect, prevent, and respond to fraud or security incidents
- Comply with legal obligations
- [Other product-specific uses]
```

**GDPR-required addition — lawful basis for each purpose:**

```
For users in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland, we 
rely on the following lawful bases under GDPR Article 6:

- Performance of contract: providing the services you signed up for
- Legitimate interests: service improvement, fraud prevention, marketing to 
  existing customers (where applicable)
- Consent: marketing communications, optional analytics, cookies that aren't 
  strictly necessary
- Legal obligation: compliance with applicable laws
```

## Section 4 — How we share information

```
We share information with:

- Service providers / subprocessors who help us operate the service (hosting, 
  payment processing, analytics, customer support, communication tools). 
  See [subprocessor list URL].

- Other users of the service (only as part of normal service use, e.g., shared 
  workspaces, team features).

- Authorities, when legally compelled (court order, subpoena, regulatory 
  request), with notice to the affected user where legally permitted.

- Affiliates of the company (parent / subsidiary entities), where applicable.

- A successor in interest in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale 
  of substantially all assets.

We do not sell personal information for monetary compensation.
[If you "sell" or "share" under CCPA's broad definitions, you must say so 
explicitly here and provide opt-out mechanism.]
```

## Section 5 — Cross-border data transfer

For GDPR compliance:

```
CROSS-BORDER TRANSFERS

If you are located outside [United States / your primary jurisdiction], your 
information may be transferred to and processed in [our primary jurisdiction]. 
We rely on the following mechanisms for cross-border data transfers:

- Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission for 
  transfers from the EEA to third countries
- [Adequacy decision, if applicable]
- [Binding Corporate Rules, if applicable]
- [Other mechanisms as applicable]

You may request a copy of the safeguards in place by contacting us.
```

## Section 6 — Retention

```
RETENTION

We retain information for as long as necessary to provide the services and 
fulfill the purposes described in this policy, including:

- Account information: for the duration of your account plus [X] days/months 
  after deletion
- Transactional records: as required by applicable accounting and tax law 
  (typically 7 years in the US)
- Logs: typically 12-24 months
- Backups: as part of our standard backup retention schedule (typically 30-90 
  days)

You may request deletion of your information at any time, subject to legal 
retention requirements.
```

## Section 7 — Your rights

```
YOUR PRIVACY RIGHTS

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

- Access the information we hold about you
- Correct inaccurate information
- Delete your information ("right to be forgotten")
- Restrict or object to certain processing
- Receive a copy of your information in a portable format
- Withdraw consent (where consent is the lawful basis)
- Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

EEA/UK USERS: Your data subject rights under GDPR/UK GDPR. We will respond 
within one month (extendable to three for complex requests).

CALIFORNIA USERS: Your rights under CCPA/CPRA. To exercise rights, [contact 
mechanism]. We will respond within 45 days (extendable to 90).

[Add other jurisdictions as applicable.]

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at [privacy@example.com]. We may 
need to verify your identity before responding.
```

## Section 8 — Cookies and tracking

```
COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

We use cookies and similar technologies for:

- Essential functions (authentication, security, basic site operation)
- Preferences (your settings and choices)
- Analytics (understanding how the service is used)
- Marketing (where consent is given)

For details on the specific cookies we use and to manage your preferences, 
see our [Cookie Notice URL] or use the cookie banner on the site.

EU users: we obtain consent for non-essential cookies via the cookie banner.

CA users: you can opt out of cookies that constitute "selling" or "sharing" 
of personal information under CCPA via the cookie banner or [Do Not 
Sell/Share My Personal Information link].
```

## Section 9 — Security

```
SECURITY

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your 
information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

[List your major measures at a high level — encryption in transit, encryption 
at rest, access controls, periodic security testing, etc.]

We hold [SOC 2 Type II / ISO 27001 / other certifications, if any]. Reports 
may be available under NDA to existing customers on request.

No system is fully secure. If we discover a security incident affecting your 
information, we will notify you in accordance with applicable law.
```

## Section 10 — Children

```
CHILDREN

Our service is not directed to children under 13 (US) or 16 (EEA/UK, where 
applicable). We do not knowingly collect information from children below those 
ages. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact 
us and we will delete it.
```

---

## Section 11 — Changes to this policy

```
CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted here with 
an updated "Last updated" date. For material changes, we will provide additional 
notice (email or in-product notification).

Your continued use of the service after a change constitutes acceptance, 
subject to your right to delete your account.
```

## Section 12 — Specific addenda (add as applicable)

### EU/UK GDPR Addendum
- Lawful basis disclosure (in Section 3 above)
- Data Protection Officer contact (if applicable / required)
- EU representative (Article 27)
- Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority

### CCPA/CPRA Addendum (CA users)
- Categories of personal information collected (in last 12 months)
- Categories of sources
- Purposes of use
- Categories of third parties with whom information is shared
- Whether you "sell" or "share" information (and opt-out mechanism)
- Sensitive personal information disclosures + opt-out

### HIPAA (if you handle PHI)
- Notice of Privacy Practices (separate document required by HIPAA)
- BAA signed with covered entity
- Restrictions on use and disclosure
- Patient rights

### Children's Online Privacy (COPPA — if directed at <13 US children)
- Parental consent mechanism
- Data minimization disclosures
- Verifiable parental consent

## When to call a privacy attorney (RED tier)

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Data breach or suspected breach | Stop, document, call privacy + breach counsel |
| Regulator inquiry (FTC, EU DPA, state AG) | Stop responding to the regulator until counsel-cleared |
| Material expansion into EU / UK / Brazil / India | Counsel review of policy + DPA + data architecture |
| First B2B customer with custom DPA terms | Counsel review of their DPA |
| HIPAA-covered data first time | HIPAA attorney + BAA template |
| First time handling sensitive personal info (health, biometric, geolocation, etc.) | Counsel review |
| First time using sensitive AI / automated decision-making | EU AI Act counsel |
