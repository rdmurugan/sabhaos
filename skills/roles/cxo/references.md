# CXO — references & further reading

Customer experience / retention / customer success references.

---

## Foundational

- **McClure, D.** "Startup Metrics for Pirates: AARRR" (2007, multiple iterations). The pirate metrics funnel.
- **Ellis, S.** *Hacking Growth* (with Brown, 2017). Operator-grade growth + retention.
- **Reichheld, F.** "The One Number You Need to Grow." *Harvard Business Review*, December 2003. The original NPS paper.
- **Reichheld, F. & Markey, R.** *The Ultimate Question 2.0* (2011). Updated NPS methodology + close-the-loop discipline.

## Activation + onboarding

- **Bush, W.** *Product-Led Growth* (2019). PLG framework with activation as central concept.
- **Sherman Lee on activation** (multiple essays). Operator-grade activation analysis.
- **Mason, J.** *The Inflection Point*. Activation rate analysis.
- **Reforge / Brian Balfour** essays on activation and growth loops.

## Retention + churn

- **Skok, D.** SaaS Metrics 2.0 (forentrepreneurs.com). Cohort retention math, NRR, LTV.
- **a16z's "16 Startup Metrics" and "16 More Startup Metrics."** Foundational metric definitions.
- **Lin Wang on cohort analysis** — practitioner essays.
- **Mixpanel + Amplitude product analytics guides** — methodology + visualization for cohort analysis.

## Customer success

## On the durability of these frameworks

The AARRR funnel and the four-kinds-of-churn taxonomy have proven durable across decades of operator practice. The specific tools (Mixpanel vs. Amplitude, Intercom vs. Front, etc.) change; the frameworks don't.

Specific industry benchmarks (activation rates, retention curves) age slowly but worth re-verifying every 1-2 years against fresh data.
