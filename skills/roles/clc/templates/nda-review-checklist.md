# NDA review checklist — template

Most NDAs are GREEN-tier and can be reviewed in 5-10 minutes without an attorney. This checklist makes that review systematic.

**Tier escalation:** if you find more than two YELLOW marks, escalate the NDA to attorney review before signing.

---

## Section 0 — Pre-read scan (30 seconds)

```
Counterparty (legal entity name + state of formation):  [   ]
Mutual or unilateral:                                    [mutual / unilateral]
Length (pages):                                          [   ]
Term / duration of obligations:                          [   ]
Governing law:                                           [   ]
Date received / response deadline:                       [   ]
```

If unilateral or longer than 3 pages, expect more YELLOW flags. Read more carefully.

---

## Section 1 — Mutual or unilateral

```
☐ GREEN — Mutual. Both parties bind to confidentiality.
☐ YELLOW — Unilateral but you're clearly the only one disclosing (e.g., investor doing 
   diligence on you). Acceptable in context.
☐ YELLOW — Unilateral and you're the receiver but they have a lot to share too.
   Push for mutual.
```

---

## Section 2 — Definition of confidential information

```
☐ GREEN — Defined as "information marked confidential or that a reasonable person
   would understand to be confidential"
☐ GREEN — Defined by category (technical, financial, strategic, etc.)
☐ YELLOW — Defined as "all communications" or "any information exchanged"
   (too broad — captures normal social conversation)
☐ YELLOW — Requires confidentiality markings AND no oral disclosures count
   (too narrow — accidental disclosure loses protection)
```

---

## Section 3 — Standard carve-outs (must be present)

The receiving party should be allowed to use / disclose information that is:

```
☐ Already known to them before disclosure (independent prior knowledge)
☐ Independently developed without using the confidential information
☐ Available in the public domain through no fault of the receiver
☐ Disclosed to them by a third party with no confidentiality obligation
☐ Required to be disclosed by law / court order (with notice to discloser)
```

**Missing carve-outs → YELLOW.** This is a standard set; an NDA without them is overreaching.

---

## Section 4 — Duration

```
☐ GREEN — 2-5 years for general confidential information; perpetual for explicitly
   defined trade secrets.
☐ YELLOW — Indefinite for ordinary confidential information (too broad; modern law
   typically caps general confidentiality at 5-7 years).
☐ YELLOW — Less than 1 year (too short — does this protect anything meaningful?).
☐ RED — Survives termination forever AND includes carve-outs as undefined "trade
   secrets" without justification.
```

---

## Section 5 — Permitted use / purpose

```
☐ Defined narrowly (e.g., "to evaluate a potential business relationship")
☐ The receiver agrees to use the confidential info only for that purpose
☐ Restrictions on who within the receiving organization can access (need-to-know)
```

**Watch for:** broad "use for any business purpose" — eliminates much of the protection.

---

## Section 6 — Embedded non-solicit / non-compete

```
☐ Carefully scan for any clause restricting hiring, soliciting, or competing
☐ If present: this is NOT a standard NDA — it's a hybrid agreement
☐ Non-solicit terms vary in enforceability by jurisdiction
☐ California: most non-competes are unenforceable; some carve-outs apply
☐ Other states: enforceability varies
```

**If a non-solicit or non-compete is embedded:** YELLOW. Separate it from the NDA discussion and negotiate independently. Don't sign an NDA that handicaps your hiring strategy.

---

## Section 7 — Remedies / damages

```
☐ GREEN — Standard "equitable relief without bond" + monetary damages
☐ YELLOW — Liquidated damages with a specific number (negotiate; this is unusual)
☐ YELLOW — Indemnification for breach (overreach; mutual indemnity is fine, but
   one-sided indemnity is not)
☐ RED — Personal guarantees from individual signatories (unusual; refuse)
```

---

## Section 8 — Return / destruction

```
☐ Material returned or destroyed on demand or termination
☐ Right to retain certain materials (e.g., for legal recordkeeping, archival
   backup) — standard exception
☐ Time limit on destruction (e.g., 30 days)
```

---

## Section 9 — Governing law and jurisdiction

```
☐ GREEN — Mutual preference (often the discloser's home state)
☐ YELLOW — A jurisdiction neither party is in (consider why)
☐ YELLOW — Plaintiff-friendly jurisdictions for IP / contract disputes (e.g., 
   Eastern District of Texas) — be aware
☐ Mandatory arbitration vs court — both work; preference is operator-dependent
```

---

## Section 10 — Signature mechanics

```
☐ eSignature explicitly allowed (or implicitly via "counterparts" + electronic 
   acceptance)
☐ Title of signatories (must be authorized to bind the legal entity)
☐ Counterpart signing allowed
```

---

## Final tally

```
Count YELLOW flags:  [   ]
Any RED flags:       [yes / no]

Recommendation:
  0-2 YELLOW, no RED:   GREEN — operator signs after a fresh read of the full document
  3+ YELLOW:            YELLOW — redline the problematic sections; counter-propose
  Any RED:              RED — do not sign; attorney review essential
```

---

## Standard redline positions (when you need to negotiate)

| Issue found | Counter-proposal |
|---|---|
| Unilateral when it should be mutual | "We propose mutual confidentiality given that both parties will share information in this discussion." |
| Indefinite duration | "We propose 3 years from the date of disclosure for general confidential information, with perpetual protection for trade secrets specifically identified as such." |
| Embedded non-solicit | "We propose handling employment / hiring restrictions in a separate agreement if needed; this NDA should be limited to confidentiality." |
| Missing standard carve-outs | "We propose adding the standard carve-outs (prior knowledge, independent development, public domain, third-party disclosure, legally compelled disclosure)." |
| Catch-all "all communications" definition | "We propose limiting confidentiality to information marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand as confidential, with specific categories listed." |
| Plaintiff-aggressive jurisdiction | "We propose [neutral or our home jurisdiction] as governing law, given that this is a mutual confidentiality agreement." |

---

## When to skip the checklist entirely

For unmistakably routine NDAs:

- One-page, mutual, 3-year term, with standard carve-outs
- Peer counterparty (similar size, similar industry, no asymmetric power)
- No embedded non-solicit / non-compete
- Standard remedies, mutual jurisdiction

Sign without running the full checklist. The cost-benefit doesn't favor heavy review.

**But:** if anything about the counterparty or context feels unusual, run the checklist anyway. Better one extra 10-minute review than one overlooked signature that locks you out of a hire two years later.
