## The Real Question You Should Be Asking

> *Why is a 2-year client 40 days out on a $90K invoice?*

**Benign explanations:**
- AP process slowdown, new approval layers

**Concerning explanations:**
- They're in financial trouble
- Relationship has shifted and nobody told you
- They're stalling because of a dispute they haven't voiced

**At 40 days you genuinely don't know which this is yet**, and that matters more than the credit line math.

## The Credit Line Math (Straight Answer)

$40K at 12% APR ≈ **$400/month in interest**

That's not the problem. The problems are:

- You don't actually need it *yet* based on your runway
- Taking it doesn't recover your $90K
- It can create a **false comfort** that reduces urgency on the invoice
- If the invoice is truly at risk, $40K doesn't solve a $90K problem anyway

## The invoice play

**Day 1 (today):** CEO-to-CEO or CEO-to-CFO call. Not email. Two years of relationship earns you a direct conversation. Say: *"The invoice is 40 days out. I need to understand what's blocking it."*

Three likely answers:

2. **They're having their own cash problem** → negotiate: 50% now, 50% in 30 days. Partial is better than a line of credit.
3. **There's a dispute you didn't know about** → now you know. Deal with it before day 60, when this gets ugly.

**Day 3 (if no commitment):** Written notice that work pauses at day 60 outstanding unless a payment plan is in place. Not a threat — a policy. Put it in writing.

**Day 7 (if still nothing):** Loop in a collections attorney for a demand letter. In most jurisdictions, the letter alone moves 70%+ of stuck invoices.

## On the line of credit

Don't close the door on it — **apply now, draw never** (or draw only if the invoice craters past 90 days with no resolution). Getting approved takes time. Approval costs nothing. Drawing costs 12%.

If the invoice goes uncollected entirely, your effective runway drops to 3.5 months and *then* you take the line while you replace the revenue. But that's a different decision on different facts.

---

## The tradeoff

Taking the line now costs ~$400–500/month and signals to yourself that you've accepted the invoice is lost. Chasing it costs you one hard phone call. Make the call.
