# Playbook — your first hire

For founders making their first hire. The most consequential single hire of the company's life; gets disproportionate strategic attention.

---

## Pre-decision (1-2 weeks)

```
□ Confirm WHY you're hiring
   - What capacity are you missing?
   - What revenue / roadmap is blocked without this?
   - Can the founder do it for 6 more months? If yes, do that first.

□ Confirm WHAT role first
   - Engineering vs. design vs. product vs. sales vs. ops
   - If unclear, the founder is the bottleneck — that's the role
   - Common first hires:
     - Technical founder → first hire often a generalist engineer or designer
     - Business founder → first hire often a technical co-founder or senior engineer
     - Solo + product-led → first hire often customer success / support / community

□ Confirm runway permits
   - Loaded cost of FTE ~ $150-250K/year for senior, $100-150K junior in US
   - Plus 3 months of ramp + 6 months of severance buffer
   - Total commitment ~ $200-300K of runway

□ Define the role
   - 1-page JD with first-90-day deliverables
   - If you can't write the JD, you're not ready to hire
```

---

## The role-design discipline

The first hire's role should be:

```
□ Well-defined enough that you can describe their first 90 days
□ Owned enough that they have real authority (not "help the founder")
□ Sized for one person (not 3 jobs squeezed into one)
□ Critical enough to matter (not "nice to have")
□ Generalist enough to absorb unexpected work (early-stage reality)
```

**Common operator mistake**: hiring "an engineer to help with everything." That's not a role; that's a vague hope.

---

## Sourcing (2-4 weeks)

For your first hire, the operator network is the highest-quality source.

```
□ Make a list of 20 people in your network who match the role
□ For each, note: relationship, current employment, perceived availability
□ Reach out personally to top 5-10:
   "I'm building [thing]. I need a [role]. I think you'd be exceptional or 
   know someone exceptional. Could we chat for 20 minutes?"
□ Ask each one for 2-3 referrals (multiplier on your reach)
□ Public sourcing channels (warm > cold):
   - LinkedIn (existing connections; outbound to 2nd-degree)
   - Twitter / X (DMs, founder communities)
   - Founder communities (YC, On Deck, indie hackers, etc.)
   - Specific role networks (Read.cv, Hatchways, etc.)
```

**Anti-patterns for first hire:**

- Posting on Indeed / LinkedIn Jobs and waiting (rare to get great signal)
- Recruiter outsourcing (you're not yet investable enough to delegate this)
- Hiring someone you don't really trust because they want to join (founder optimism)

## Interview process for first hire (5-7 stages, 2-3 weeks)

This is the most important hiring process you'll run. Take it seriously.

```
1. Founder + candidate intro call (30 min)
   - Mutual fit + interest
   - Light skill probe
   - Mutual decision to proceed

2. Practical work session (2-4 hours, paid if >2 hours)
   - Real task they'd do in the role
   - You observe how they think + work
   - They observe how you operate

3. Backchannel reference (15-20 min each, 2-3 people)
   - Former managers, peers, reports
   - Both founder-asks AND candidate-arranges

4. Co-founder / advisor call (30-60 min)
   - Outside perspective on the candidate
   - Important if you don't have a co-founder

5. Mutual dinner / coffee (1-2 hours)
   - Long-form chat about life, values, motivations
   - Don't skip this for first hires

6. Reverse interview (45-60 min)
   - Candidate asks tough questions of you / company
   - Their questions reveal their thinking quality

7. Decision call (30 min)
   - Make offer or clear no
```

**Don't drag it out beyond 3 weeks.** Great candidates have options; lengthy processes lose them.

## Compensation for first hire

The first hire's comp is unusually leverage-d (high equity, lower base, founder-adjacent commitment).

```
First hire compensation (operator-stage, pre-seed to seed):

Base:    50-75% of market rate (e.g., senior engineer at $140-180K vs $200K+ market)
Variable: usually none (no sales role)
Equity:  0.5% - 2.5%
   - 2.5%+ if they're effectively a co-founder (consider true founder status instead)
   - 1.0-2.0% for first senior hire in a specialized role
   - 0.5-1.0% for first non-senior hire
   - All on standard 4-year vest with 1-year cliff
Benefits: Standard package (health, 401k, equipment, PTO)

Total cash compensation: typically $130-200K loaded for first hire
```

**The conversation**: be honest. *"This is below market on base because we're early. The equity makes the math work if the company succeeds. Here's our funding plan and our path to market-rate comp."*

## Reference checks (the most-skipped step)

For the first hire, this is non-negotiable. Do at minimum 2-3 reference calls.

```
For each reference:
□ Ask about specific projects (concrete, not generic)
□ Ask about working style
□ Ask about how they responded to disagreement
□ Ask about how they handled failure / setbacks
□ Ask: "If you could hire them again, would you?"
□ Ask: "Is there anything you wish you'd known before hiring them?"

Two source rule:
- 1-2 references arranged by the candidate
- 1-2 backchannel references (people you find independently)

Backchannel often catches things candidate-supplied references miss.
```

## The offer (operator-grade)

```
□ Verbal offer first (call, share the numbers, gauge reaction)
□ Written offer within 24 hours
□ Offer letter:
   - Per the offer-letter-checklist.md template
   - Clearly states: at-will (US), IP assignment requirement, equity terms
   - 7-14 day acceptance window
□ Equity grant pending board approval (don't promise specific strike — depends on 409A)
□ Pre-onboarding: send Slack / Notion / Google Workspace invites
```

## Onboarding (the first 90 days)

```
DAY 1 (Friday ideal):
□ Equipment + accounts ready
□ Schedule for first week (1:1s, walk-arounds, intro to existing customers)
□ Welcome lunch with founder

WEEK 1:
□ 1:1 with founder daily
□ Walk through entire codebase / product / market context
□ Meet 3-5 customers (if B2B) or do customer-call observation
□ Ship one small thing by end of week

MONTH 1:
□ Shipped something meaningful (the first big-ish thing they own)
□ Met everyone they need to know
□ 30-day check-in: how's it going, what surprises, what's hard
□ Adjust on what's revealed in the conversation

MONTH 3:
□ Performing the role independently
□ 90-day FIT DECISION explicitly made (both sides)
□ If working: equity grant fully approved + 409A locked
□ If not working: have the hard conversation, don't drag it out
```

## What can go wrong (and what to do)

| Pattern | Action |
|---|---|
| 30 days in, founder is still doing 80% of the role | Either the JD was wrong or the hire isn't right. Investigate. |
| Hire is great but you can't tell because there's no scope | Define scope this week. "Own X, deliver Y by date." |
| Hire isn't working out at 60 days | Difficult conversation. Either fix or part ways. Don't drag past 90. |
| Hire is amazing but unhappy | Investigate what's broken — comp, working style, role definition, founder dynamic. Often fixable. |
| Hire wants more equity / more title 3 months in | Decide deliberately. Common pattern; depends on actual contribution + your runway. |

## Hand-off triggers

| Situation | Specialist |
|---|---|
| International first hire | Use EOR (Deel, Remote) to avoid entity setup. International employment counsel for >5 hires/country. |
| First hire is technical co-founder caliber | Consider founder status (more equity, different commitment) over employee status. CLC review of equity grant. |
| First hire failed PIP / fit issue | CHRO + CLC for severance package + release. |
| First hire was a founder mistake (hired too fast) | Honest reflection + lessons learned. Don't repeat. |

---

## The strategic context

The first hire signals what kind of company you are:
- First-hire engineer → "we're an engineering-led company"
- First-hire designer → "we're a craft / quality-led company"
- First-hire BD / sales → "we're a market-led company"
- First-hire ops / customer success → "we're a service / operations-led company"

Pick deliberately. The first 5 hires set the culture; the next 50 reinforce it.
