# CSO — references & further reading

Strategy / competitive / partnership / market-entry references.

---

## Strategy fundamentals

- **Porter, M.** "What Is Strategy?" *Harvard Business Review*, November 1996. The canonical "strategy is choosing what NOT to do" essay.
- **Porter, M.** *Competitive Strategy* (1980). Generic strategies (cost leadership / differentiation / focus); industry analysis.
- **Lafley, A.G. & Martin, R.** *Playing to Win: How Strategy Really Works.* Harvard Business Review Press, 2013. The "5 strategic questions" frame (where to play, how to win, etc.) — underpins REFERENCE §1.
- **Rumelt, R.** *Good Strategy / Bad Strategy.* Crown Business, 2011. Diagnosis + guiding policy + coherent action; sharpest distinction between strategy and goals.

## Competitive positioning

- **Trout, J. & Ries, A.** *Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind.* (1981 / 2001 annotated). Foundational positioning text (CMO cross-reference; CSO uses category positioning).
- **Moore, G.** *Crossing the Chasm.* HarperBusiness, 1991/2014. Technology adoption lifecycle; category positioning for B2B tech.
- **Dunford, A.** *Obviously Awesome.* Ambient Press, 2019. Operator-grade positioning (CMO crossover with CSO category work).

## Disruption + market evolution

- **Christensen, C.** *The Innovator's Dilemma.* HarperBusiness, 1997. Disruption theory; how incumbents lose to disruptors.
- **Christensen, C.** *The Innovator's Solution* (with Raynor). 2003. Operator response to disruption.

## Blue ocean / category creation

- **Kim, W.C. & Mauborgne, R.** *Blue Ocean Strategy.* Harvard Business Review Press, 2004. Category creation vs. category entry.
- **Ramadan, A., Peterson, D., Lochhead, C., & Maney, K.** *Play Bigger: How Pirates, Dreamers, and Innovators Create and Dominate Markets.* HarperBusiness, 2016. Category design framework.

## Scenario planning + uncertainty

- **Wack, P.** "Scenarios: Uncharted Waters Ahead." *Harvard Business Review*, September 1985. The original Shell scenario planning method.
- **van der Heijden, K.** *Scenarios: The Art of Strategic Conversation.* Wiley, 2005. Practical scenario planning.
- **Klein, G.** "Performing a Project Premortem." *Harvard Business Review*, September 2007. Pre-mortem method for big bets (cross-references CFO and CLC).

## Game theory + competitive dynamics

- **Brandenburger, A. & Nalebuff, B.** *Co-opetition.* Doubleday, 1996. Game theory applied to competition + cooperation.
- **Bensaou, M. & Earl, M.** Articles on competitive intelligence and strategic-fit framing.

## Mergers, acquisitions, partnerships

- **Christensen, C. et al.** "The Big Idea: The New M&A Playbook." *Harvard Business Review*, March 2011. M&A success patterns.
- **Damodaran, A.** *Damodaran on Valuation* and his free corporate finance resources. For M&A valuation.
- **Feld, B. & Mendelson, J.** *Venture Deals.* Wiley. M&A from the perspective of someone selling a company.

## Operator strategy

- **a16z essays on strategy** (a16z.com/category/strategy).
- **First Round Review on strategy** (firstround.com/review). Many operator-grade pieces.
- **Reforge / Brian Balfour** on growth strategy specifically.
- **Y Combinator essays** by Paul Graham (paulgraham.com) and others. Particularly "Do Things That Don't Scale" and the growth essays.
- **Stratechery / Ben Thompson** for strategic analysis of tech companies.

## Operating cadence + execution

- **Doerr, J.** *Measure What Matters.* OKR methodology.
- **Bossidy, L. & Charan, R.** *Execution: The Discipline of Getting Things Done.* Crown Business, 2002.
- **Andy Grove** *High Output Management.* Random House, 1983/2015. Operating cadence + strategic clarity.

## Investor / board perspectives

- **Reid Hoffman's *Blitzscaling*** for hypergrowth strategic considerations.
- **Bill Gurley's blog** (abovethecrowd.com) on competitive dynamics in tech.
- **Brad Feld** on board governance, strategy at startup-stage.
- **Mark Suster's blog** (bothsidesofthetable.com).

## Cognitive biases in strategic decisions

- **Kahneman, D.** *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Strategic decision biases (anchoring, sunk cost, planning fallacy).
- **Lovallo, D. & Kahneman, D.** "Delusions of Success: How Optimism Undermines Executives' Decisions." *Harvard Business Review*, July 2003.

## On the durability of these frameworks

Strategy frameworks are more durable than tactical playbooks. The frames in REFERENCE.md (3-question filter, wedge analysis, scenario planning) have survived 40+ years of market change because they're about decision structure, not specific market dynamics.

Specific market dynamics (current category positioning, competitor lists, market-size estimates) age quickly. Refresh at least annually; refresh major strategic documents whenever market conditions shift meaningfully.
