## Section 3 — Channel maturity classification

Classify each active channel:

| Channel | Stage | Read |
|---|---|---|
| [   ] | Learning (< 3 months) | Don't judge yet; observe |
| [   ] | Optimizing (3-9 months) | Identify lever; iterate on creative/audience/bid |
| [   ] | Scaling (9-18 months) | Increase budget; watch for saturation |
| [   ] | Saturated (CAC creep > 30% in 90 days) | Diversify off; preserve current spend at the efficient level |
| [   ] | Declining (cohort retention or CAC trend worsening) | Investigate; decide to fix or kill |

**A mature portfolio has channels at multiple stages.** Only-learning is unstable. Only-scaling means no future bets. Aim for at least one each at learning, optimizing, and scaling.

## Section 4 — Allocation by stage

### Pre-PMF (< $250K ARR, no consistent acquisition)

```
Goal: find one working channel + one compounding bet
Recommended:
  Paid search:   40-60% of budget (fastest signal)
  One social:    20-30% (LinkedIn for B2B; Meta for B2C)
  Content / SEO: 20-30% (compounds; start now even if signal lags)
Risk profile: high concentration acceptable; you're searching for fit
```

### Early growth ($250K - $2M ARR)

```
Goal: diversify off the first working channel; defend against saturation
Recommended:
  Original winning channel: 40-50% (don't kill it; just don't grow it as fast)
  Second paid channel:      15-25% (cross-test audiences and creative)
  Content / SEO:            15-25% (now compounding; lean in)
  Outbound (if ACV >$30K):  10-20%
  Partnerships seeded:      5-10% (long-payback experiments)
Risk profile: diversify aggressively; no channel should exceed 60% of pipeline
```

### Growth ($2M - $10M ARR)

```
Goal: build defensive moats; reduce supplier risk on paid channels
Recommended:
  Paid (combined):     30-40%
  Content / SEO:       20-30% (this is your moat)
  Outbound:            15-25%
  Partnerships:        10-15% (now generating meaningful pipeline)
  Events / community:  5-10%
Risk profile: highest-paid channel < 30% of pipeline; brand starts compounding
```

### Scale ($10M+ ARR)

```
Goal: optimize for unit economics within a diversified portfolio
Recommended: ICP-segmented allocation; different mix per segment
```

## Section 5 — Adding a new channel — the 12-week protocol

Before adding any new channel:

```
□ Hypothesis written: who you'll reach, what message, why this channel
□ Budget committed: minimum $1K-2K/week for paid; equivalent in time for organic
□ Measurement plan: how you'll attribute and at what cadence (weekly cohorts)
□ Decision date: +12 weeks from start (no judging earlier — channels need cohort data)
□ Kill criterion: specific CAC threshold above which we stop
□ Scale criterion: specific CAC threshold below which we 2× budget
```

If you can't fill all six in 30 minutes, you're not ready to add the channel.

**The 12-week rule:** don't kill a paid channel before 12 weeks (unless it's catastrophically broken). Don't kill an organic channel before 6 months. Channels have learning curves; impatience kills good channels.

---

## Section 6 — When to kill a channel

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| CAC > 3× blended for 8+ consecutive weeks | Kill |
| Cohort retention from this channel materially below other channels | Kill (worse than high CAC) |
| Channel is at 60-70% of payback window AND below 50% of expected efficiency | Kill |
| Channel CAC creeping >30% in 90 days (saturation) | Preserve current spend; don't scale; investigate audience expansion |
| Channel CAC stable but pipeline volume insufficient | Add a second channel; don't try to scale this one past saturation |

**Sunk-cost trap:** "we've spent $X on this channel." Past spend is irrelevant. Decide on forward-looking efficiency.

---

## Section 7 — The portfolio review cadence

| Cadence | Activities |
|---|---|
| **Weekly** | CAC by channel, pipeline by channel, kill/scale decisions on running experiments |
| **Monthly** | Channel-cohort retention; portfolio rebalance if any FAIL diagnostics; new channel hypothesis review |
| **Quarterly** | Strategic portfolio shift if stage transition (PMF → growth, etc.); add/retire major channels |
| **Annually** | Full attribution audit; refresh of allocation by stage |
