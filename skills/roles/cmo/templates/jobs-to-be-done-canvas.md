# Jobs-to-be-Done canvas — template

The Christensen frame: customers don't buy products, they hire products to do a job. Filling this canvas forces you to articulate the job in customer-language, not product-language.

---

## Section 1 — The job statement

```
When I [trigger / circumstance],
I want to [motivation / goal],
so I can [desired outcome / progress].
```

Example:
> When I'm asked a load-bearing strategic question by a founder I advise,
> I want to give them a decisive recommendation grounded in real frameworks,
> so I can earn their trust and they can stop hedging on the next move.

The job statement is in the customer's voice and describes a *progress* they're trying to make, not a feature of your product.

---

## Section 2 — The four forces of progress

Why customers SWITCH (push) and why they DON'T (pull):

| Force | Description | Your evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Push of the current situation** | Pain with their existing solution. The strongest force. | [What's specifically broken about their current workaround?] |
| **Pull of the new solution** | Promise of better progress. | [What outcome do you uniquely offer?] |
| **Anxiety of the new** | Fear of switching cost or being wrong. | [What anxieties block adoption? Migration cost? Learning curve? Reputational risk?] |
| **Habit of the current** | Inertia. Familiar workflows. | [What habits / muscle memory do they have that your product disrupts?] |

**The strongest marketing maximizes the first two forces and minimizes the second two.** Generic marketing only does pull (features) and ignores push, anxiety, and habit.

## Section 5 — The progress moment

```
The moment they hire your product is when [specific concrete moment].

Before that moment, they were [describing the world without you].
After that moment, they are [describing the world with you].
```

Example:
> The moment they hire Sabha is when they ask a load-bearing question and need a decisive answer, not a survey of options.
> 
> Before, they spent 20 minutes filtering through Claude's "five things to consider" replies and still had to make the call alone.
> 
> After, they get a routed answer with a recommendation, a tradeoff, and a next step — in 2 minutes.

This is the **scene** marketing should evoke. Not "AI productivity"; the specific moment of relief.

---

## Section 6 — Hire and fire rules

| | Hire (use the product) | Fire (drop the product) |
|---|---|---|
| **Triggers** | [What makes them start using you?] | [What makes them stop?] |
| **Mediators** | [Who decides? Who advocates?] | [Who pushes back?] |
| **Required outcome** | [What proves it works?] | [What proves it doesn't?] |

The "fire" column is the most-skipped and most-important section. Knowing why customers churn tells you what your marketing must prove pre-purchase.

---

## Section 7 — Translation to marketing

Now translate the JTBD into actual marketing copy:

| Marketing artifact | JTBD-grounded version |
|---|---|
| **Hero headline** | Should evoke the job + the progress, not features |
| **Sub-headline** | Should name the trigger or the push |
| **First CTA** | Should match the urgency of the trigger |
| **Three feature blocks** | Each should address one of: pull, anxiety reduction, habit-bridging |
| **Social proof** | Should show customers BEFORE/AFTER the hiring moment |
| **Pricing page** | Should anchor on the cost of the alternative (workaround), not just the price of the product |

---

## Section 8 — JTBD validation interviews

Before trusting this canvas, validate with 5-10 customer interviews. Questions:

```
1. Walk me through the day you decided to start using [our product]. What 
   was happening that morning? What had you tried before?

2. What other solutions were you considering at that moment? What made you 
   pick us specifically?

3. After you started using us, what did you stop doing that you used to do?

4. If we disappeared tomorrow, what would you do instead?

5. Have you ever almost stopped using us? What was happening then?
```

The patterns in 5+ interviews will validate, refine, or replace your canvas. **Always validate before scaling on a hypothesis.**
