# CEO — references & further reading

Synthesis / founder-level decision-making references.

---

## Decision-making under uncertainty

- **Kahneman, D.** *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* Foundational on judgment biases.
- **Klein, G.** "Performing a Project Premortem." *Harvard Business Review*, September 2007.
- **Kahneman, D., Lovallo, D., & Sibony, O.** "Before You Make That Big Decision." *Harvard Business Review*, June 2011. Twelve-question checklist for major decisions.
- **Duke, A.** *How to Decide* (2020). Annie Duke's framework on decisions under uncertainty.
- **Dweck, C.** *Mindset.* On the role of mindset in decision-making.

## Founder mode

- **Graham, P.** "Founder Mode." Essay, September 2024 (paulgraham.com).
- **Grove, A.** *High Output Management.* Foundational management text.
- **Horowitz, B.** *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* (2014). Operator-grade CEO decisions.
- **Catmull, E.** *Creativity, Inc.* CEO of Pixar on managing creative companies.

## Leadership and synthesis

- **Bezos, J.** Annual shareholder letters (1997-present). Free at sec.gov. Decisive-action / regret-minimization / two-way doors framework.
- **Drucker, P.** *The Effective Executive* (1967). Time management, decision quality, leadership.
- **Christensen, C.** *How Will You Measure Your Life?* (2012). Long-horizon decision-making, regret framework.

## Board / investor management

- **Feld, B. & Ramsinghani, M.** *Startup Boards.* Operator-grade board management.
- **Wilson, F.** AVC blog (avc.com). Board management, fundraising dynamics.
- **Mark Suster's blog** (bothsidesofthetable.com). Investor-side perspective.

## Pivots, exits, and major transitions

- **Blank, S.** *The Startup Owner's Manual* (with Dorf). Pivot framework.
- **Eisenmann, T.** *Why Startups Fail* (2021). Pattern recognition on failure modes.
- **Vaynerchuk, G.** various — operator-grade on personal commitment to a venture.
- **HBR articles on selling companies** — multiple.

## Cognitive biases in CEO decisions

- **Lovallo, D. & Kahneman, D.** "Delusions of Success: How Optimism Undermines Executives' Decisions." *Harvard Business Review*, July 2003.
- **Bazerman, M. & Moore, D.** *Judgment in Managerial Decision Making.* (Academic but operator-relevant.)
- **Tetlock, P.** *Superforecasting.* On calibration, particularly relevant to long-horizon decisions.

## Founder psychology / wellness

- **Brad Feld + Jerry Colonna** writings on founder mental health.
- **Reboot.io** (executive coaching firm) blog.
- **Lara Hogan — Resilient Management.** Operator-grade leadership.

## Operator perspectives on CEO work

- **Lenny's Newsletter** (lennyrachitsky.com).
- **First Round Review** (firstround.com/review). Many essays on founder / CEO topics.
- **Founders Podcast** (David Senra) — biographical study of operators.
- **Stratechery** (Ben Thompson) on strategic positioning of major companies.

## On the limits of frameworks

The CEO role exists for situations that don't reduce to frameworks. Most CEO decisions involve:

- Judgment under irreducible uncertainty
- Values + identity, not just optimization
- Personal commitment, not just analytical correctness
- Long-horizon decisions where today's frameworks may not apply tomorrow

The references above provide *patterns* for thinking, not *answers*. The actual decisions remain the founder's.

---

## A note on the CEO role's intentional simplicity

This skill is deliberately lighter than CFO, CMO, CLC. The CEO doesn't compete with functional depth — the CEO integrates it. Three pages of frameworks beat thirty pages of advice. When in doubt, ask: "What's the irreducible question, and whose call is it?"
