# Playbook — repositioning

For when current positioning is wrong (not stale — wrong). High-cost move: a real repositioning costs 6-12 months of compounding lost. Only do this when the data justifies it.

---

## Step 0 — Confirm it IS a positioning problem

Repositioning is over-recommended as a fix for problems that are actually:
- **Distribution** (right people, no awareness) — fix channels, not message
- **Message** (right positioning, wrong copy) — rewrite landing pages, not the position
- **Product** (positioning is fine; product doesn't deliver) — fix product first
- **Pricing** (positioning is fine; price elasticity is broken) — fix price

Diagnostic checklist:

```
□ Customer interviews: do 5+ customers describe you in language that matches your positioning?
   - If YES: positioning is landing; problem is elsewhere
   - If NO: positioning is the problem; continue this playbook
   
□ Inbound quality: when prospects come to you, do they understand what you do without explanation?
   - If they ask "are you like [X]?" → positioning is broadcasting wrong signal
   - If they describe themselves as ICP and ask intelligent questions → positioning is working

□ Sales calls: at the end, do prospects describe what they're buying in your words or theirs?
   - In theirs: positioning is reframable; fixable with messaging
   - In neither (vague) → positioning is the issue

□ Win-rate by ICP segment: is there a clear "we win when we're for X" pattern?
   - If yes: position TIGHTER on X; don't reposition entirely
   - If no: maybe genuine ICP confusion

□ Lost-deal language: when you lose, do prospects say "we ended up going with X because Y"?
   - Y reveals what they thought they were buying — compare to your positioning
```

If 3+ of these point to positioning, proceed. If <3, fix the closer problem first.

---

## Step 1 — Customer language audit (weeks 1-2)

You can't reposition without hearing customer language. Run:

```
□ 10 customer interviews (current customers)
   Questions:
   - Walk me through why you bought us. What were you looking for?
   - What did you compare us to? What made us win?
   - How do you describe us to a peer who hasn't heard of us?
   - What's the most underrated thing about us? Most overrated?

□ 5 lost-deal interviews (prospects who didn't buy)
   Questions:
   - What were you trying to solve?
   - What did you end up doing instead?
   - What was the deciding factor against us?
   - If we built [X], would you reconsider?

□ 5 prospect interviews (still evaluating)
   Questions:
   - What problem are you trying to solve?
   - What have you tried before?
   - What would 'great' look like for you?
```

20 interviews. 2 weeks. The patterns in their language become the input to repositioning.

---

## Step 2 — Find the pattern (week 3)

Pull the transcripts. Tag every customer phrase by category:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **The push (what's broken with their current state)** | "I was tired of...", "We kept running into..." |
| **The pull (what they wanted)** | "I needed something that...", "We were looking for..." |
| **The category they put you in** | "It's like X, but...", "We thought of you as..." |
| **The anxiety (what almost stopped them)** | "I was worried about...", "We were nervous that..." |
| **The win condition (what 'good' meant)** | "Now we can...", "It means we don't have to..." |

The strongest *push* and the strongest *pull* phrases that recur across 5+ interviews are your repositioning material.

---

## Step 3 — Draft new positioning (week 4)

Use the [positioning statement template](../templates/positioning-statement.md). Three drafts minimum:

```
Draft A: "tightest possible ICP"
   Most narrow target, sharpest category, polarizing differentiation

Draft B: "current ICP but new category"
   Same target, but reframe the category so the comparison set is more favorable

Draft C: "new ICP but adjacent category"
   Shift target slightly to where the customer language was strongest
```

Run each through the five tests in the template. Pick one. Then iterate.

---

## Step 4 — Stress test with prospects (week 5)

Before committing, validate:

```
□ Show 5 fresh prospects (haven't seen your existing positioning) the new draft
□ Ask: "What do you think this product does? Who is it for? Who would NOT buy this?"
□ Listen for:
   - Are they describing the right job?
   - Are they self-identifying as ICP if they fit?
   - Are they self-rejecting if they don't fit? (polarization is good)
□ Iterate the positioning based on what they actually heard, not what you meant
```

If 4 of 5 prospects describe what you intend the product to do, the positioning lands. If <3 do, iterate or pick a different draft.

---

## Step 5 — Internal alignment (week 6)

Before going public, the whole team needs to understand and commit:

```
□ Founder: signs off on the new positioning publicly and in 1:1s
□ Sales: re-learns the demo, the discovery script, the objection responses
□ Product: confirms the product can deliver on the new promise (push back if not)
□ Customer success: knows how to talk to existing customers about the change
□ Engineering: aware of the change (affects roadmap priority)
□ Everyone: can recite the new positioning statement from memory by week's end
```

If any role can't articulate the new positioning, you'll get team-level inconsistency that confuses customers. Internal alignment is a gate, not a checkbox.

---

## Step 6 — External rollout (weeks 7-10)

Sequence matters:

```
Week 7 — soft launch:
□ Homepage hero rewritten
□ "About" page rewritten
□ Founder LinkedIn / X profiles updated
□ First content piece in new positioning published
□ Sales call openers updated

Week 8 — broaden:
□ All landing pages and feature pages aligned
□ Outbound email templates updated
□ Sales decks rewritten
□ Demo scripts updated

Week 9 — communicate to existing customers:
□ Email to all customers: "we're sharpening how we describe what we do; here's what's changing and what it means for you"
□ Most existing customers won't care; the ones who do will appreciate transparency
□ Provide answers to predictable questions: "is the product changing?" (no), "is the price changing?" (separate decision), "does this affect my contract?" (no)

Week 10 — broader announcement (optional):
□ Blog post / public note explaining the repositioning rationale (the "why" is interesting; the "what" is just operational)
□ Social media thread with the new positioning + reasoning
□ Press / community outreach if the repositioning is strategically important
```

---

## Step 7 — Measure (weeks 11+)

Set 30/60/90 day reviews:

| 30 days | 60 days | 90 days |
|---|---|---|
| Sales call qualifier hit-rate | Demo-to-close ratio by source | Inbound volume |
| "How did you find us" language | Lost-deal reasons | Brand search volume |
| Outbound reply rate | New-customer NPS | Customer language convergence |

**The 90-day check is the real verdict.** Conversion improvement at 30 days could be coincidence. At 90 days, it's signal.

---

## When to abort / revert

You'd revert (rare) if:
- 90-day conversion is materially worse (>20% drop) AND no other factor explains it
- The new positioning attracts wrong-fit customers who churn at 2× the rate
- The team can't internalize the new positioning (internal misalignment becomes external confusion)

You'd iterate (more common) if:
- Conversion is similar to before — the new positioning isn't worse but isn't better either; refine, don't revert
- One segment responds well, another doesn't — consider ICP refinement, not full revert

---

## Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| Repositioning every 6 months | Compound positioning over years, not months |
| Marketing-led repositioning (founder/CEO absent) | Founder must sign; positioning IS strategy |
| Skipping customer interviews | Without customer language, you're guessing |
| Internal positioning without external validation | Test with prospects before going live |
| "We'll let the market figure out the new positioning" | The market needs you to tell it. Position deliberately. |
| Repositioning to chase a trendy category | Categories cycle; you'll be misaligned in 18 months |

---

## When the answer is "don't reposition"

After running this playbook, the answer is often:
- Sharpen ICP within current positioning (most common)
- Rewrite landing pages without changing positioning (~30% of cases)
- Fix distribution (~20% of cases)
- Fix product gap (~10% of cases)
- Actually reposition (~10-20% of cases)

The playbook is valuable even when the conclusion is "don't reposition." The diagnostic work surfaces the real problem.
